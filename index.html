Here's an overview of our project.

Problem (Why are we doing this?)

As HIV remains a significant public health concern in the Philippines, many still lack knowledge and hold misconceptions about HIV transmission and prevention methods despite ongoing efforts to educate the public.

Without schools and communities talking openly about sex and health, lots of women in the Philippines don't know much about HIV/AIDS or how to stay safe. Because of this, they might do things that put them at risk without realizing it, and they might not know what they can do to protect themselves.

Solution (What's our plan?)

Research Questions

Is there any correlation between one’s education level and their perception of HIV?

What are other factors affecting one’s perception of HIV and other STIs?

Null Hypothesis

One’s educational background does not significantly influence their perception of HIV

Alternative Hypothesis

Educational background is a determining factor in shaping one’s perception of HIV

Action Plan

Analyze the content of the PiNUT HIV Dataset that focuses on how women in the Philippines perceive HIV in relation to their educational background.

Data Collection

How did we collect our data?

Description of Dataset, Data Collection Process, and Dataset Size

The dataset is part of a larger dataset, the 2022 Philippines National Demographic and Health Survey (NDHS). The 2022 NDHS is a nationwide survey with a nationally representative sample of about 35,000 households from 34,232 housing units. Our dataset primarily focuses on basic demographic indicators, health status, and the knowledge and attitudes regarding HIV/AIDS. Thus, we are working with sample survey data. The information collected through the NDHS is intended to assist policymakers and program managers in designing and evaluating programs and strategies for improving the health of the country’s population.

Sampling Method

The sampling scheme provides data representative of the country as a whole, for urban and rural areas separately, and for each of the country’s administrative regions. The first stage involved a systematic selection of 1,247 primary sampling units (PSUs) distributed by province or HUC. A PSU can be a barangay, a portion of a large barangay, or two or more adjacent small barangays. In the second stage, an equal take of either 22 or 29 sample housing units were selected from each sampled PSU using systematic random sampling.
